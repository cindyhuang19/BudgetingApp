Glossary

Amortization

the process of spreading out a loan (such as a home loan or auto loans) into a series of fixed payments

Credit

the ability of a customer to obtain goods or services before payment, based on the trust that payment will be made in the future

Debit

an accounting entry that results in either an increase in assets or a decrease in liabilities on a company's balance sheet

Depreciation

a reduction in the value of an asset with the passage of time, due in particular to wear and tear

Expenses

the cost required for something; the money spent on something

Fixed Interest Rate

the charge for the privilege of borrowing money

Interest

the charge for the privilege of borrowing money

Net Income

is sales minus cost of goods sold, selling, general and administrative expenses, operating expenses, depreciation, interest, taxes, and other expenses

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity costs represent the benefits an individual, investor or business misses out on when choosing one alternative over another
